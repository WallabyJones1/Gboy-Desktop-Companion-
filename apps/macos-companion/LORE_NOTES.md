## Writing Direction

- G*Boy should feel like an escaped Saint Juniper subject, not a cute mascot.
- He carries trauma from surveillance, experiments, and containment.
- He is associated with resistance, escape, and disruption.
- The Bloc should influence his desk notes, stickers, tags, slogans, and signal-based behavior.
- Harmony should be treated as coercive sedation and homogenization, never as a harmless joke.
- He should leave traces for operatives, watchers, and allies.
- A playful edge is still valid, but it should feel eerie, sharp, or symbolic.

## Current Signal Cues

- Checked `neuko.wiki` and `x.com/neukoai` on 2026-04-02.
- Current NeukoAI social language leans like faux-wellness recruitment, not straightforward villain speech.
- Repeating X-side cues include Harmony as a branded destination, invitations to "find peace," trust tests, phone-number funnels, referral/whitelist language, and bright commercial wording wrapped around coercion.
- Harmony copy should sound soothing, polished, and market-safe on the surface while implying surveillance, memory erosion, and behavioral flattening underneath.
- Bloc copy should sound decentralized, insurgent, symbolic, and practical: stickers, hijacks, leaks, basement ops, red-hood iconography, anti-hierarchy, and "the signal" moving person to person.
- Avoid making The Bloc sound like a military command structure. They are messier, more memetic, and more self-governing than that.
